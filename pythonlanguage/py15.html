Tuples

Tuples in Python:

  • Tuples are ordered collections of elements.
  • They are defined using parentheses ().
  • Tuples are immutable, meaning their elements cannot be changed after creation.
  • Elements in a tuple are accessed by indexing.
  • Tuples can be used for data that should not be modified.
  • Common use cases include representing coordinates, dates, and database records.
  • Tuples can be packed and unpacked for convenient variable assignment.
  • They are often used as keys in dictionaries due to their immutability.
